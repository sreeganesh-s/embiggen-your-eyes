The Sun

The heart of our Solar System

About the Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of our solar system and the source of all life on Earth. It is a massive, glowing ball of hot plasma, primarily composed of hydrogen and helium. Its gravity holds the entire solar system together, and its light and energy drive Earth's climate and weather.

Quick Facts

  • Type: G-Type Main-Sequence Star (Yellow Dwarf)
  • Age: ~4.6 billion years
  • Distance from Earth: ~149.6 million km
  • Temperature: 5,500°C (surface), 15 million°C (core)
  • Diameter: 1.39 million km

Major Missions

  • Parker Solar Probe (NASA)
  • Solar and Heliospheric Observatory (SOHO)
  • Solar Orbiter (ESA/NASA)
  • ADITHYA-L1 (INDIA-ISRO)
